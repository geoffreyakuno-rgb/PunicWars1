First Punic War (264–241 BCE)

The First Punic War was fought between the Roman Republic and the Carthaginian Empire over control of Sicily and Mediterranean dominance. Initially, Rome lacked a navy, but adapted quickly, creating ships equipped with the corvus boarding bridge that allowed their infantry to board enemy vessels.

Major Naval Battles

  • Battle of Mylae (260 BCE): Rome’s first major naval victory using the corvus.
  • Battle of Cape Ecnomus (256 BCE): One of history’s largest naval battles; enabled Rome’s invasion of Africa.
  • Battle of the Aegates Islands (241 BCE): Decisive Roman victory ending the war.

Outcome

Carthage ceded Sicily, paid indemnities, and lost naval supremacy. Rome became the dominant naval power of the Western Mediterranean.
