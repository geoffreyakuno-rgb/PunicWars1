Third Punic War (149–146 BCE)

The Third Punic War saw the final destruction of Carthage. After years of tension and Rome’s demands to disarm, Carthage resisted, leading to a brutal siege.

Key Engagements

  • Siege of Carthage (147–146 BCE): Scipio Aemilianus besieged and ultimately destroyed the city.
  • Battle of Nepheris: Removal of outer strongholds securing Rome’s victory.

Outcome

Carthage was completely destroyed; its people enslaved and its territory became the Roman province of Africa. Rome eliminated its last rival in the western Mediterranean.
